Motivation

The Chester Square Neighborhood Association is set on providing its residents with the best living enviroment and community possible. Located on Massachusetts Avenue, the community and its park are split by one of the busiest roadways in Boston. The park is a gorgeous greenspace for the paeople of Boston, primarily those in the greater Chester Square area. Luckily for residents, there are 4 major busses which trek through the area: silver lines 4 and 5 and bus route 1 and 43. It goes without saying that many Bostonians have come to expect delays taking the MBTA. With this in mind and new capital projects in the cities pipeline, such as the Boston Rapid Transit (BTR), we aim to examine the reliability of the 4 busses which allow the citizens of Boston to venture to this park.

Our team anticipates to see that the busses running through this park (Silver Line 4, Silver Line 5, Route 1, and Route 43) expereiences many delays, especially during rush hour. We hope to be wrong in this assumption, but if it is true, we expect to have a good justification for the Boston Rapid Transit (BTR).

Task Analysis

Summary of task table.

Overall, we aimed to adress three major tasks through our project. First, worked to provide a way for Chester Square Neighborhood residents and other Bostonians alike to gain a sense of the busses which could be taken too and from the park. This includes, but is not limited to, finding bus stops near them, finding the lines near them, and undertanding what times of day expereicne the most delays. Second, we wanted to analyze the delays of busses over an extended period of time in order for commuters to gain a better understanding of the trends in interuptions in their travel. Third, our team strived to show the difference between the scheduled location and the physical locations of the busses through the various visualizations.
